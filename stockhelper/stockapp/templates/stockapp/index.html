Click on a card to learn each term.

Equity

Also known as stocks, a measure of ownership of a company's assets

Debt

Also known as bonds, a loan given to a company that must be paid back at some point

Mutual Fund

An active, or managed, investment to purchase stocks from a fund manager

Index

A measure of how a group of stocks are doing

Index Fund

A passive investment to purchase stocks that follow a certain index

ETF

Also known as an exchange-traded fund, an investment similar to mutual funds, except that stocks can be traded throughout the day, instead of at the end of the day

Futures Contract

An agreement to buy or sell an asset at a specified price and future date

Trader

A person requesting to buy or sell assets

Market Exchange

A system where goods and services are exchanged

Broker

A person that handles transactions between the trader and the exchange

Market Order

A trade that's executed immediately after a deal is made

Limit Order

A trade that's executed at a certain price or better

Stop-Loss Order

A trade that becomes a market order once a certain price is reached

Stop-Limit Order

A trade that becomes a limit order once a certain price is reached

Volume

A measure of how much a stock has been traded after a certain period of time

Share Price

The value of one stock

Market Cap

The value of a company's shares (share price * # of shares)

EPS

Also known as earnings per share, the amount a company earns per outstanding share

P/E Ratio

Also known as the price-earning ratio, the ratio of a company's earning to the share price (share price / EPS)

Portfolio

A collection of investments held by a trader

ROI

Also known as return on investment, the amount of profit made on an investment

Risk

The probability of incurring a loss from an investment

Volatility

A measure of fluctuations in the stock price

Liquidity

A measure of cash a company has
